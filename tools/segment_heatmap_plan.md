# Segment Heatmap Backtest Framework (Multi-Risk Edition)

## Goal
Design and run a robust 3-month backtest framework that historically simulates the new "Segment Heatmap Strategy". The backtest must evaluate the system at 15-minute intervals, applying a 5x leverage profile. 

Instead of assuming ATR is the best stop-loss metric, we will simulate **4 distinct Risk Management profiles** concurrently. By the end of the 3-month run, we will know exactly *which* type of SL works best for *which* segment.

## Methodology

### 1. The Historical Simulation Engine
- Fetch 3 months of 15-minute historical candle data for the top coins in all 8 segments.
- Pre-compute all HMM features.
- Iterate through time chronologically.

### 2. The Segment Heatmap Logic (The "Router")
For every 15-minute tick:
1. **Compute Heat:** Calculate the 24h rolling `volume_zscore` and normalized `volatility` for all segments.
2. **Rank & Select:** Choose the top 2 "hottest" segments.
3. **Execute HMM:** Feed the active segment coins into their corresponding Segment-Specific `HMMBrain`.
4. **Generate Signals:** If `BULL`, go Long. If `BEAR/CRASH`, go Short.

### 3. Multi-Risk Manager Testing (The Lab)
For every single trade generated by the HMM, the framework will simulate its outcome against **4 distinct SL methodologies** simultaneously to record which method yielded the highest PnL and Win Rate for that specific segment:

1. **Static Percentage SL:** (e.g., 5% away from entry). Traditional, fast, but blind to market structure. Will likely get hunted in Memes but might work for L1s.
2. **Dynamic ATR Trailing SL:** (e.g., 2.5x the 14-period Average True Range). Breathes with volatility. If the coin is chopping wildly, the SL widens.
3. **Market Structure SL (Swing Low/High):** Places the stop below the lowest low (or highest high) of the previous 10 candles. Highly logical but can result in massive initial risk if the previous candle was a huge wick.
4. **HMM Regime-Shift Exit (No Hard SL):** The purest test of the HMM. We do not set a rigid SL; we only exit the trade when the HMM actively predicts the regime has shifted (e.g., exiting a Long when the engine flips from BULL to CHOP or BEAR). 
5. **Advanced Trailing & Partial TP:** (The "Scale-Out" Manager). Sets a mathematically defined SL (e.g., 1.5 ATR). Upon hitting Target 1 (e.g., +2%), it moves the Stop Loss to Breakeven (Entry Price) and books 50% of the position. It then trails the remaining 50% using a trailing stop (e.g., keeping the SL 2% behind the highest high reached) to capture the "fat tail" runners without giving back initial profits.

### 4. Trade Management & PnL
- **Leverage:** 5x Leverage on the theoretical balance.
- **Fees:** Simulate a Binance standard 0.04% taker fee on entry and exit.

### 5. Analytics & Output
At the end of the simulation, we will output a comprehensive grid matrix:

**A. Risk Management Validation:**
For each Segment (L1, AI, Meme, etc.), display the performance of all 5 Risk Managers:
- Total Net PnL (%)
- Max Drawdown (%)
- Sharpe Ratio
- Win Rate (%)
- Risk/Reward Ratio

**B. System Latency & Operations Reporting:**
- The average and maximum **Execution Time (in seconds)** required to run the `MultiTFHMMBrain` models during the simulated 15-minute cycles based on the number of shortlisted coins actively trading.

**C. Feature & Model Analytics (Overfitting/Underfitting Validation):**
- **Model Confidence Variance:** Does the model constantly predict 99% or 33% confidence? A model that flips wildly between 33% and 99% implies underfitting/noise.
- **Useless Coins List (Exclusion Hitlist):** A definitive list of coins that systematically generated negative PnL or random walk results across all 5 risk managers. These coins are mechanically "useless" to the HMM algorithm and will be permanently moved to `config.COIN_EXCLUDE`.
- **Feature Importance Mapping:** A re-validation of which specific features from `segment_features.py` actually contributed to the winning trades vs losing trades during the simulation.
